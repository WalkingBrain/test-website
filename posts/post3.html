How to measure time?

This might seem like a weird question, but hear me out. I live in Czechia, which means I'm one hour ahead of Greenwich. My clocks also follow daylight saving time, meaning once a year, I get one hour more sleep, and once a year one hour less.

I've intentionally avoided a single acronym so far: UTC. A standard by which time is measured, based on the TAI, temps atomique international (International atomic time). UTC doesn't follow daylight savings time, so during summer, you can confuse people into thinking you're in UTC-1, but in reality you're talking UTC. At the time of writing, it's 12:11 PM local time, but 10:11 AM UTC. So that's how I measure time now.
